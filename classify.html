Plant Classification

Understanding plant relationships is essential for identifying and studying various species. Plants are categorised into hierarchical groups:

Relationship Explaination

Family:
This is the highest classification group normallly reffered to. Modern classification assigns a type of plant to each family as an example of that family characteristic as distinguishable from other families.

Examples:

  1. Dipterocarpaceae
  2. Lauraceae

Genus:
This is the part of plant nomenclature that is the most familiar. Plant in a genus are easily recognizable as belonging to the same group. The name of the genus should always capitalized.

Examples:

  1. Vatica
  2. Hopea

Carl Linnaeus: The pioneer of plant taxonomy

A distinguished Swedish botanist, Linnaeus is celebrated for establishing the binomial nomenclature system, a method that remains in use today for classifying and naming plant species.

Species:
This is the level of classification that defines the individual plant. The use of the genus and species names together always refer to only one plant.

Examples:

  1. Obtusifolia
  2. Madang

Family: Dipterocarpaceae

Genus: Vatica

Species: Rassak

Vatica rassak is a species of tree belonging to the family Dipterocarpaceae, commonly found in the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia. This tree is known for its valuable timber, which is often used in construction and furniture making. Vatica rassak typically grows to a height of 30 to 40 meters and features a straight trunk with a dense canopy. The species plays a significant role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
